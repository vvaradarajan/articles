# Logical vs Physical and Dopamine


Software development has been analyzed a lot, and one usual model is the distinction between Logical and Physical. Logical is what is desired (aka ‘requirements’) and Physical is how it is done using the tools on hand. Many stages are introduced in-between => Logical - requirements - block diagrams - component breakdown - object oriented design - blah..blah.. => Physical: databases, code and finally most often: “Man! This is not what I wanted! Let’s start again!”


Yoga takes this approach a little further: Conceptual => Logical => Physical. The conceptual part is the philosophy and by restricting itself to Life (not basic elements and physics), it answers fundamental questions as truisms (Life exists because it does..) and then builds a theory of based on these and defines the rules for Life to continue to exist .. the Sutras (rules to live by), Karma (duty consisting of: the need to constantly seek knowledge as challenges to life are ever occurring), the continuity of living from generation to generation that is sustained by Karma, the impossibility of reaching the end and how to deal with it => ‘do not get too attached to the outcome, but observe..’


Now comes the Logical Model => Pranayama, pancha khosas etc. This is logical, since there is no evidence of any Yogi dissecting the physical body to find the Prana or the Khosas. Yoga simply theorizes that these must exist. Example: Prana - the life force must exist and be somewhere in the Physical model.  All the khosas must exist and must be implemented in the Physical Model. Yoga did not get into the Physical Model to the detail of ‘modern science’ and its Logical Model is sufficient, to understand many physical models (carbon based human, or silicon based life etc.). Yogic practices on the physical (pranayama, asanas, keeping balance within the khosas) are merely to keep to the rules dictated by the Logical Model.


This whole email was to get to Dopamine. Dopamine is the most important bridge between the physical and logical. That bridge leads to the logical (pleasure,happiness, sadness, satisfaction and so on..). This bridge is necessary and is key to the survival of the physical. It is the mechanism to generate action to sustain it. Without Dopamine, no action can take place (ex: The frozen addicts, the movie ‘Awakenings’ - Robin Williams on Ldopa). Yoga sticks to the logical with its description of khosas. Dopamine is one mechanism of how the khosas are implemented.  Dopamine level variation provides the motivation for continuation of life-sustaining activities.



Physical implementations are not not easy. The specific implementation using Dopamine requires its continuous variation. There is no steady state.  Too much Dopamine (too much ‘pleasure’? ) will cause inaction or madness and physically most bodily systems will shut down leading to cessation of life. Dopamine releases un-related to what happens to the physical body (i.e. create dopamine using drugs) will cause random actions and prone to damaging the body.

Too little dopamine will of course lead to in-action.  A steady level of dopamine will cause random actions unrelated to what is happening  (i.e. there is no variation in feelings) and this is not life by definition).


It is the variation in the levels of dopamine that track the body’s well-being that is essential, and this is too-complicated. Luckily the mechanisms in our bodies automatically ensure it and as long as we do not mess with it, it will work most of the time.  The rest of the time, we can use the logical reason and coordinate the variation of dopamine with things that are good for life. This again is too-complicated and perhaps AI can help us here.


The last sentence in the last paragraph begs the question: Can we by our minds just operate without the physical implementation - i.e. can we logically induce pleasure, pain, happiness, sadness etc. and thrive in life. The surprising answer is that we have always been doing this - we seek good paying jobs, take time to watch movies, exercise, play games, do good work and all of these are logical, and dopamine automatically follows.  A physical implementation is necessary, but can be created through the mind!
