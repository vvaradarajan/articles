# Eye Injury: Vasan

A tennis ball smashed into the eye at an angle coming from below.  The picture shows a diagram and the description of the injury and comments are below:

<img src="img/eyeInjury/eyeInjury.png">  

The tennis ball hit the cornea at an angle. The CT scan showed injury to the cornea and a laceration (or tear) was suspected. This is an eye emergency: 
The Aqueous humor will leak out of the cornea, and the pressure in the Anterior Chamber will drop, causing the rear Vitreous fluid gel to collapse forward and retinal detachment/distortion/optic nerve damage.. will occur. The pressure must be restored to keep the eye from collapsing...!

Opthalmalogists at the Stein Eye Institute, checked with their equipment, and did not find a laceration. What they found was a 'flattened' Anterior Chamber but no leaks of the Aqueous Humor fluid. There was trauma in the eye, and exploratory surgery indicated swelling and distortions inside, but all membranes etc were intact. Specifically the Vitreous fluid - gel, and the Aqueous humor in the Anterior chamber were intact, and no intervention was required. Vision was blurry/dim due to inflammation and distortions as well as due to ointments and tears on the external surface of the eye. It should get better as inflammation reduces and natural healing takes place.

Four weeks later (Apr 18 - event, today May 13) substantial healing has taken place. Vision after injury was 20/70 (i.e. What is normally seen at 70 ft, could only be seen when much closer at 20ft). Vision now is 20/30!  This indicates that the lens is getting its functionality back. The still slight bluriness is thought to be due to the exploratory surgery disturbing the muscles around the lens 'sac' and should improve, and needs to be checked at 2 months.

Other Notes: Flattening of the Anterior Chamber (i.e. cornea is flatter in the injured eye) was still observed. A concern was that the production and drainage of the Aqueous humor fluid could be affected. The flattening caused the 'angle' where the drainage occurs to be narrowed, making the draining the less efficient. If this persists, there is increased chances of Glaucoma (Aqueous humor accumulates and increases eye pressure). Luckily the opthalmologists (and especially Ms. Elise Ma) know their subject, and can quickly determine and treat issues if they are consulted!
